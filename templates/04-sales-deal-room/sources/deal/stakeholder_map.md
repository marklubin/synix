# MegaCorp Industries — Stakeholder Map

**Deal ID: MC-2026-0142 | Last updated: February 2026**

## Decision-Making Structure

MegaCorp uses a consensus-driven procurement process. Final vendor selection requires sign-off from the VP Analytics, CTO, and CFO. The evaluation committee includes technical evaluators from the Data Engineering and Analytics teams. Below are the key stakeholders and their priorities.

## Sarah Chen — VP of Analytics

- **Role**: Decision maker and executive sponsor
- **Reports to**: COO (Mark Stevens)
- **Team size**: 55 direct reports across 3 analytics teams
- **Background**: 15 years in analytics, previously Director of BI at Deloitte
- **Top priorities**: time-to-value (wants her team productive within 30 days), custom ML model support for her data science group, executive dashboard quality
- **Communication style**: data-driven, prefers concise presentations with clear ROI metrics
- **Relationship status**: had an introductory call on January 15, 2026; expressed strong interest in NovaSight's ML capabilities
- **Risk**: may push for aggressive timeline that could stress implementation quality

## James Rodriguez — CTO

- **Role**: Technical approver; has veto authority on any technology purchase over $100K
- **Reports to**: CEO (Patricia Hughes)
- **Background**: 20 years in enterprise IT, former VP Engineering at Boeing
- **Top priorities**: enterprise security (SOC2, SSO/SAML integration with Okta), API capabilities for integration with their ERP system (SAP S/4HANA), data residency and compliance
- **Communication style**: detail-oriented, asks probing technical questions, wants architecture diagrams
- **Relationship status**: scheduled technical deep-dive for February 20, 2026
- **Risk**: historically conservative; may favor DataFlow due to brand recognition and compliance portfolio

## Lisa Park — Head of Data Engineering

- **Role**: Technical evaluator; will lead the migration and integration work
- **Reports to**: James Rodriguez (CTO)
- **Team size**: 12 data engineers
- **Background**: 8 years in data engineering, expert in Snowflake and dbt
- **Top priorities**: migration complexity (wants automated Tableau migration tooling), data connector quality (especially Snowflake and SAP), API documentation quality
- **Communication style**: hands-on, wants to test the product herself during the POC
- **Relationship status**: attended our webinar in December 2025; asked detailed questions about Snowflake connector performance
- **Risk**: concerned about disrupting existing data pipelines during migration

## David Kim — CFO

- **Role**: Budget approver; controls the $300K-500K annual budget
- **Reports to**: CEO (Patricia Hughes)
- **Background**: Former finance VP at 3M, highly focused on cost justification
- **Top priorities**: clear ROI justification (wants to see payback within 18 months), total cost of ownership comparison across vendors, reduction in IT maintenance overhead
- **Communication style**: financial, wants spreadsheets with 3-year cost projections
- **Relationship status**: no direct contact yet; Sarah Chen will introduce us after technical demo
- **Risk**: may push for lowest-cost option; need to frame value beyond seat price

## Emily Wong — Analytics Team Lead

- **Role**: End user champion; will drive day-to-day adoption across 500 analytics users
- **Reports to**: Sarah Chen (VP Analytics)
- **Team size**: 18 analysts
- **Background**: 6 years as a Tableau power user, built 200+ dashboards for MegaCorp
- **Top priorities**: modern UX and self-service capabilities, ease of transition from Tableau, training and onboarding support, collaborative features
- **Communication style**: enthusiastic, product-focused, active in analytics community (speaks at Tableau conferences)
- **Relationship status**: connected on LinkedIn; she reached out after seeing our product demo at Analytics Summit 2025
- **Risk**: strong Tableau loyalty — needs to be convinced that NovaSight is a genuine upgrade, not a lateral move
