# DataFlow Inc — Competitor Profile

**Last updated: January 2026**

## Company Overview

DataFlow Inc was founded in 2016 and went public in 2022 (NASDAQ: DFLW). Headquartered in San Francisco, CA. FY2025 revenue was $180M ARR, representing 18% year-over-year growth. The company employs approximately 1,400 people, with 500 in engineering and 300 in sales. Market capitalization as of January 2026 is approximately $1.2B.

## Core Product

DataFlow's enterprise data platform provides end-to-end analytics — from data ingestion and transformation to dashboards and embedded analytics. The product is built for large enterprises (1,000+ employees) with complex data environments. They support 150+ native data connectors and offer a visual ETL pipeline builder.

## Pricing

- No public pricing; custom enterprise contracts only
- Typical range: $80-120/seat/month depending on volume and feature tier
- Minimum contract: $100K/year (effectively 100+ seats minimum)
- 3-year contracts common with 15-20% discount over annual pricing
- Professional services billed separately at $250/hour

## Strengths

- Comprehensive enterprise compliance: SOC2 Type II, HIPAA, FedRAMP Moderate, ISO 27001
- Deep integrations with enterprise systems (SAP, Oracle, ServiceNow, Workday)
- 24/7 premium support with dedicated Technical Account Manager for all Enterprise customers
- Robust role-based access control with row-level security
- Strong brand recognition — Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader for 3 consecutive years

## Weaknesses

- Implementation cycles average 4-6 months, with some taking up to 9 months
- Complex, dated UI — G2 reviews frequently cite steep learning curve (3.4/5 for ease of use)
- Expensive: total cost of ownership often 2-3x initial quote after professional services
- Slow release cadence — major updates quarterly, bug fixes monthly
- No AI/ML capabilities native to the platform; relies on partner integrations
- Customer satisfaction declining: NPS dropped from 45 to 32 in 2025

## Market Position

DataFlow holds approximately 22% market share in enterprise analytics (1,000+ employee companies). They are strongest in financial services, healthcare, and government verticals. G2 rating: 3.9/5 (1,240 reviews). Gartner Peer Insights: 4.0/5 (890 reviews).

## Recent Moves

- Acquired CloudMetrics (a cloud cost analytics startup) in June 2025 for $50M
- Announced a partnership with AWS for co-selling in October 2025
- Launched "DataFlow AI" in Q4 2025 — widely seen as a rushed response to AI-native competitors
- CFO resigned in December 2025; interim CFO appointed from the board
- Missed Q3 2025 earnings estimates by 8%, stock down 22% since July 2025
